Core Principles

Magnetic Field (B)

B = μ₀nI

The magnetic field inside a long solenoid is uniform and proportional to the current and turns per unit length.

Magnetic Energy (W)

W = ½LI²

Energy is stored in the magnetic field generated by an inductor when current flows through it.

Energy Density (u)

u = B² / (2μ₀)

The amount of energy stored per unit volume in a region of empty space where a magnetic field exists.

Real-world Applications

Transformers

Uses magnetic coupling to step up or step down AC voltages efficiently across power grids.

Electric Motors

Converts electrical energy into mechanical energy through interacting magnetic fields.

Wireless Charging

Transfers power across empty space via electromagnetic induction between two coils.

Power Supplies

Inductors act as filters in switch-mode power supplies to smooth out current fluctuations.
